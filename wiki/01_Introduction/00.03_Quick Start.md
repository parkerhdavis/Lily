# Quick Start

This walkthrough takes you from a fresh install to a completed client document. It should take about five minutes.

---


## Step 1: Set Your Templates Folder

Templates are the Word documents (`.docx`) that Lily uses as starting points for client documents. They live in a folder on your computer, and Lily needs to know where to find them.

1. From the **Lily Hub** (home screen), open **App Settings** via the gear icon or the grid menu (⊞)
2. Click **Browse** next to **Templates Folder**
3. Select the folder containing your `.docx` templates
4. Navigate back to the Lily Hub

If you don't have any templates yet, you can create a Word document with placeholder variables like `{Client Full Name}` and save it in your templates folder. See [Understanding Templates](wiki/04_Templates/03.00_Understanding%20Templates.md) for details.

---


## Step 2: Open a Client Folder

Each client's work lives in its own folder on your computer. This is typically a directory you'd already have — something like `Clients/Doe, Jane/`.

1. From the Lily Hub, click **Open Client Folder**
2. Select a folder for your client (or create a new one)
3. You'll land on the **Client Hub** — the home base for this client

The first time you open a client folder, Lily creates a small project file (`.lily`) to track the client's information. This happens automatically — you don't need to manage it.

---


## Step 3: Add a Document

1. In the Client Hub, click **Add New Document**
2. The **Template Picker** opens, showing your templates folder as a browsable tree
3. Click a template to select it

Lily copies the template into the client folder. You'll be taken to the **Variable Editor**, where you can start filling in the document.

---


## Step 4: Fill in Variables

The Variable Editor shows your document preview on the left and a form on the right. Each placeholder from the template — like `{Client Full Name}` or `{Date of Birth}` — appears as a field in the form.

1. Fill in the fields in the sidebar
2. Watch the preview update in real time as you type
3. Click a highlighted placeholder in the preview to jump to its field in the sidebar

Variables are shared across all documents for the same client. If you fill in `{Client Full Name}` for one document, it's already filled in for the next one.

---


## Step 5: Save

Press `Ctrl+S` (or `Cmd+S` on Mac) to save. Lily writes your values into the Word document — the resulting `.docx` file in the client folder is a standard Word document you can open, edit, and print like any other.

The "Unsaved" badge in the header disappears once your changes are saved.

---


## What's Next

You've completed your first document! Here are some next steps:

- **Add more documents** — Go back to the Client Hub and add more templates for the same client. Variables you've already filled in carry over automatically.
- **Use the Questionnaire** — For structured intake, try the [Questionnaire](wiki/02_Client_Management/01.03_The%20Questionnaire.md), which walks through client information section by section.
- **Add Contacts** — If your documents reference other people (agents, trustees, etc.), see [Contacts](wiki/02_Client_Management/01.02_Contacts.md).
- **Browse your templates** — The [Pipeline](wiki/05_Pipeline/04.00_The%20Pipeline.md) lets you browse all your templates and see which variables they use.
