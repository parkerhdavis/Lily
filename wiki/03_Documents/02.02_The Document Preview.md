# The Document Preview

Lily renders a live HTML preview of your Word document so you can see exactly what the finished product will look like as you fill in variables.

---


## What the Preview Shows

The preview faithfully reproduces the formatting from the original Word document, including:

- Paragraph structure and alignment (left, center, right, justified)
- Bold, italic, underline, and strikethrough text
- Font sizes and font families
- Text color and highlighting
- Headings (H1–H6)
- Indentation (left, right, first-line, hanging)
- Paragraph spacing (before, after, line spacing)
- Tables with borders and cell formatting
- Numbered and bulleted lists
- Page breaks
- Hyperlinks (clickable in the preview)
- Superscript and subscript
- ALL CAPS and small caps formatting

Variable placeholders are highlighted in the preview so they're easy to spot. As you fill in values in the sidebar, the highlighted text updates immediately — no need to save or refresh.

---


## Interacting with the Preview

### Click to Navigate

Click any highlighted variable in the preview to jump to its field in the variable form on the right. This is especially useful in long documents where scrolling through the form would be slow.

### Live Updates

The preview updates in real time as you type in the variable form. Formatting is preserved — if a placeholder sits inside bold text in the template, the filled-in value stays bold too.

### Case-Aware Display

The preview respects the original casing of each placeholder occurrence. If `{Client Full Name}` appears in title case in one spot and `{CLIENT FULL NAME}` appears in all caps elsewhere, each occurrence shows your value in the appropriate case.

---


## Conditional Variable Preview

For conditional variables, the preview shows whichever text branch is currently active based on the checkbox state. Toggle the checkbox in the form and watch the preview switch between the two options.

---


## Limitations

The preview is a high-fidelity approximation, but it's rendered as HTML rather than in Word's native layout engine. A few things may look slightly different from the final `.docx`:

- **Exact page breaks** — The preview shows page break indicators, but line wrapping and pagination may differ slightly from Word
- **Images** — Embedded images in templates are not yet rendered in the preview
- **Complex table layouts** — Merged cells and nested tables may render with minor differences

The saved `.docx` file is always the authoritative version. When in doubt, open it in Word to check the final layout.
